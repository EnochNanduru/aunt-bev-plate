Kintsugi-Inspired Plate Restoration

Embracing imperfection and celebrating repair as part of an object's unique history.

What is Kintsugi?

Kintsugi (金継ぎ, "golden joinery"), also known as Kintsukuroi (金繕い, "golden repair"), is the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery by mending the areas of breakage with lacquer dusted or mixed with powdered gold, silver, or platinum. It treats breakage and repair as part of the history of an object, rather than something to disguise. The philosophy highlights the beauty in imperfection, transforming a damaged piece into something new and often more valuable.

History & Wabi-Sabi

Kintsugi is said to have originated in the late 15th century when Japanese shogun Ashikaga Yoshimasa sent a damaged Chinese tea bowl back to China for repairs. When it returned mended with unsightly metal staples, Japanese craftsmen sought a more aesthetically pleasing method of repair, giving birth to Kintsugi.

This art form is deeply connected to the Japanese philosophy of wabi-sabi, which embraces the flawed or imperfect. Kintsugi doesn't just fix an object; it celebrates its scars as a unique part of its journey, often making the repaired piece more beautiful and resilient than before.

Our Project: Aunt Bev's Plate

This project is inspired by a real-life mishap with a cherished family item. My Aunt Bev has a beautiful Talavera plate, a treasured souvenir from a memorable trip to Mexico. One fateful day, it slipped and tragically broke into four distinct pieces. Heartbroken but determined not to discard such a meaningful piece, we decided to embrace the Kintsugi philosophy and give it a new life, highlighting its history rather than hiding the damage.

The Repair Steps

  1. 1 & 2. Dry Fit & Clean Edges

    Carefully dry-fit the broken pieces to understand how they align, then thoroughly clean all broken edges with rubbing alcohol to ensure a strong bond.

  2. 3. Mix Epoxy & Gold Powder

    Prepare your adhesive by mixing a strong epoxy resin with gold mica powder according to the epoxy's instructions, achieving a consistent golden paste.

  3. 4. Apply Epoxy & Join First Piece

    Apply a thin, even layer of the gold epoxy mixture to the edges of one of the larger broken pieces and carefully join it to its corresponding part.

  4. 5. Join Remaining Pieces

    Systematically continue applying epoxy and joining the remaining fragments, including any small slivers, using masking tape to hold pieces if needed.

  5. 6. Allow Full Cure

    Once all pieces are assembled, allow the epoxy to cure completely as per the manufacturer's instructions (typically 24 hours), ensuring the plate is undisturbed.

  6. 7. Highlight Seams & Varnish (Optional)

    After full curing, you can further highlight the golden seams with more gold powder mixed with varnish, or simply apply a clear varnish for protection.

Tools & Materials

  • Broken ceramic item
  • Strong epoxy resin (clear, slow-setting recommended)
  • Gold mica powder (or other metallic powder)
  • Mixing sticks
  • Small disposable mixing cups or surfaces
  • Masking tape
  • Rubbing alcohol or acetone for cleaning
  • Paper towels or soft cloths
  • Disposable gloves
  • Fine paintbrush (optional, for highlighting)
  • Clear varnish (optional, for sealing)
  • Safety glasses
